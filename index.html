General Manual

Raredon Lab General Manual

A guide for all lab members, covering lab culture, expectations, authorship, mentorship, and more.

About This Manual

This manual applies to all members of the Raredon Lab, regardless of training level or scientific background. It covers the values and expectations that shape how the lab works: what it means to join, what is expected at each stage of training, how authorship and credit are handled, how mentorship is structured, and what leaving looks like.

Read it when you join. Consult it throughout. Return to it when questions arise. If something is unclear or you disagree with what you find here, raise it directly with the PI.

Sibling Manuals

This manual is one of three maintained by the Raredon Lab. Each covers a distinct domain, and together they apply to all lab members across all types of work.

The Computational Lab Manual covers coding standards, data practices, environment setup, version control, and computational workflows. If you work with code, data, or computational tools, that manual is required reading.

The Wet Lab Manual covers laboratory protocols, reagent and equipment standards, experimental record-keeping, and safety procedures specific to bench work. If you work at the bench, that manual is required reading.

Where content in one manual is relevant to another, cross-links are provided. This general manual takes precedence on matters of authorship, credit, lab culture, and mentorship.
